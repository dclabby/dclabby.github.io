About

Osaka, 2019

I'm Darragh Clabby from Dublin, Ireland. I studied as a mechanical engineer, completed a PhD in marine renewable energy, and have worked for companies developing wave & tidal energy conversion technologies. Recently I completed a Higher Diploma in Computing with specialization in Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning. This page gives an overview of my professional experience, education, and personal interests.


Experience

Physical model testing of OpenHydro's Open Centre Turbine at IFREMER, 2017

I've worked for companies developing technology in the fields of wave energy and tidal energy. In both cases I was involved in developing physics based numerical models in Matlab to quantify energy yield and structural loading. Furthermore, I was responsible for validating those models by implementing physical scale model tests and/or analyzing data from ocean deployed full scale systems.

Resolute Marine Energy | Lead Hydrodynamics Engineer

  • Responsible for development of the wave energy converter’s offshore systems (prime mover, power take-off, foundation);
  • Developed Matlab based numerical models used to assess dynamic response, performance and structural loading of wave energy converter;
  • Managed test campaigns to validate models following successful grant application to fund test facility access (worth €50k);
  • Led preparation for Resolute Marine’s technical demonstrator project in Gran Canaria, Spain (towards achievement of TRL 6);
  • Prepared design documentation (use cases, design basis, verification cross reference matrix, FMEA, etc.) for critical systems;

OpenHydro | Senior Turbine Analyst

  • Responsible for tidal energy resource assessment, energy yield & structural load forecasting;
  • Developed OpenHydro’s first dynamic/probabilistic numerical model; resulted in improved assessment of energy yield & extreme/fatigue loads; led to reduction in design conservatism via risk based approach;
  • Identified EU funding opportunity (€100k) to validate dynamic model through physical scale model tests; led successful application and subsequently managed implementation of test campaign; completed first laboratory scale physical model tests ever implemented by OpenHydro;
  • Developed Matlab tools to monitor and analyse data sets from operational turbines; implemented root cause analysis pertaining to observed issues and unanticipated behaviour;
  • Introduced & implemented methodology to quantify tidal energy yield uncertainty based on IEC wind energy standard;


Education

Representation of a irregular waves in the frequency & time domains

I studied as a mechanical engineer at University College Dublin from 2002 until 2006, and graduated with a first class honours degree. Following my undergraduate I pursued Master's degree involving the development of electromechanical systems for a facility used to simulate daylight in architectural scale models. In 2009 I was awarded a Marie Curie Research Fellowship to pursue a Phd in Queen's University Belfast. I graduated in 2013 and have been working in the field of marine renewable energy ever since. Recently I undertook and completed a part time course at National College of Ireland and received a Higher Dimploma in Computing with specialization in Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning.
